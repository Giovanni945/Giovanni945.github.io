Diabetes

Di·a·be·tes

A disease in which the body’s ability to produce or respond to the hormone insulin is impaired, resulting in abnormal metabolism of carbohydrates and elevated levels of glucose in the blood and urine.

Diabetes affects how food is turned into energy in your body. Food gets broken down into sugar that goes into your blood, triggering the pancreas to release insulin so glucose can enter cells and be used for energy.

There is no cure for diabetes yet, however, it is definitely manageable by taking care of one's health. This includes diet, exercise, following medication instructions, seeking support, and attending doctor appointments.
